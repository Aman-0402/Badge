# Public Sponsor

## What It Is
Awarded for sponsoring an open-source developer or organization through GitHub Sponsors. One-time badge, no tiers.

## How to Earn It

1. Go to [github.com/sponsors](https://github.com/sponsors)
2. Find a developer or project to sponsor
3. Choose a monthly amount (minimum varies, often $1–$5/month)
4. Complete payment setup
5. Badge appears on your profile automatically

## Step by Step

1. Visit [github.com/sponsors/explore](https://github.com/sponsors/explore)
2. Browse by category or search for a tool/library you actually use
3. Click **Sponsor** on their profile
4. Select a tier → click **Sponsor $X/month**
5. Enter payment details
6. Done — badge appears within minutes

## Who to Sponsor

Sponsor tools you actually use and benefit from:

- A library in your package.json or requirements.txt
- A VS Code extension you use daily
- An open-source project from a maintainer who helped you

Good starting points:
- [github.com/sponsors/explore](https://github.com/sponsors/explore)
- Look for the ❤️ Sponsor button on any repo page

## Tips

- $1/month is enough to earn the badge — the amount does not affect the badge
- You can cancel after one month if needed
- Sponsoring shows the heart icon on your profile and theirs
- Some sponsors offer perks at higher tiers (private repos, Discord access, etc.)

## What Does NOT Count

- One-time donations outside GitHub Sponsors
- Sponsoring via Patreon, Buy Me a Coffee, or other platforms
