# Galaxy Brain

## What It Is
Awarded when your answer in a GitHub Discussions Q&A thread is marked as the accepted answer. Has four tiers.

## Tiers

| Tier | Accepted Answers Required |
|------|--------------------------|
| Bronze | 1 |
| Silver | 2 |
| Gold | 8 |
| Platinum | 16 |

## How to Earn It

1. Find a repo with **Discussions** enabled and a **Q&A** category
2. Someone posts a question in Q&A
3. You post a helpful answer
4. The question author (or repo maintainer) clicks **Mark as answer** on your reply

You cannot mark your own answer — someone else must do it.

## Easiest Method (With a Friend)

1. Enable Discussions on your repo (Settings → Features → Discussions)
2. Ask a friend/collaborator to post a Q&A question
3. You answer it thoroughly
4. They click **Mark as answer** on your reply → you get Galaxy Brain

## Finding Real Opportunities

Search for unanswered Q&A discussions:

```
https://github.com/search?q=is:open+category:Q%26A&type=discussions
```

Or look for repos with active discussions in topics you know well:
- React, Vue, Python, TypeScript, Node.js
- Your own tech stack

## Tips

- Detailed answers are more likely to get marked — include code examples where relevant
- Answer questions where you actually know the solution
- Popular repos with Discussions: `microsoft/vscode`, `vuejs/vue`, `tiangolo/fastapi`
- The badge may take a few hours to appear after the answer is marked

## What Does NOT Count

- Answers in repos where the discussion category is not Q&A
- Answers marked by yourself
- Comments that are not in the "Suggest an answer" field
